Madonna's Material Girl

2nd June 2023

I am honestly awed by Madonna's evergreen song Material Girl which I picked up in a resurgence thanks to some insta trend. Drawing inspiration from Diamonds Are a Girl's Best Friend which was performed by none other than Marilyn Monroe, Madonna has set the stage to be the Material Girl of the era.

It isn't surprising that this song along with Like a Virginlaunched the career of Madonna. The song video starts with a conversation between two men in the industry discussing how she could be a major star that just might have transcended into the real world. The director shown in the video is interested in her and we get to see two sides of Madonna through his chase which adds the layer the song is about. There is reel Madonna dressed in a pink jeweled with diamonds much of what the song is about and then there is real Madonna unimpressed by the gifts that belong to her reel version.

The Day the Music Died

28th May 2023

I will admit I don't understand the fabled 'American Pie' by Don McLean but I sincerely love it. This 8-minute saga of a song has been hailed as one of the best songs in country music through generations. One has to give it to McLean for the creativity the song sparks with an almost incomprehensible story. Who is driving the Chevy to the levee? Who is the widowed bride? What about the day the music died? A relatively dry critical analysis is coming your way.

Champions of Bechtel Test

3rd May 2023

A while ago, I was searching for some simulating TV show and came across Gilmore Girls. I was familiar with the creator Amy Sherman Palladino's other more recent work The Marvelous Mrs. Maisel which without a doubt was the most colorful show I have watched. It turns out Gilmore Girls was the precedent of Mrs. Maisel for the style of dialogue. The characters love talking for long periods and it really shows the depth. Spanning across 3 generations of women and set in an imaginary small town called Stars Hollow the show excels in giving the perfect 'Spring Vibe'. It is always spring in the show!

The main characters of the story are the mother-daughter duo both named Lorelai but the daughter has taken up the pet name Rory to avoid confusion. We see the journey of Rory's teenage years and Lorelai's 30s pursuing love and life. The core of the show is really the conversation between these two sharp-wit women filled with intense humor and pop references loaded with ironical banter seemingly unknown to others. There is also Lorelai's mom Emily which makes up for the third generation who is an upper class women with privileges Lorelai had aversion to.
